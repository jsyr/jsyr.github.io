About me:

I am currently a senior Researcher at Center for Research and Technology, Hellas (CERTH) working in the area Novel Network Architectures for the Future Internet, where i primarily design and develop networking systems solutions (currently working on Information Centric Networks and Software Defined Networks). I love building things that work! and most of my publication record is about novel system implementations. I work on the team of Professor Leandros Tassiulas. I received my Phd from University of Thessaly in 2009 under the supervision of Professor Spyros Lalis.

Education:

  • 2003-2009: Ph.D. in Computer Engineering; University of Thessaly (Intern at University of California, Riverside in 2008)
  • 1998-2003: B.Sc.in Computer Engineering; Technical University of Crete; Greece

Hands-on Expertise:

During my Phd have focused on Systems Middleware(for OS core, Network I/O, Low-level Graphics) and Computer Architecture technologies. As a senior researcher i have focused on networking systems. I have worked extensively in the linux kernel, i am fairly experienced with the internals and i closely follow its evolution to date. I have also worked on hardware-software co-design approaches where i developed gateware systems using Vhdl, Verilog and the Xilinx toolchain. I have used a variety of FPGA platforms but primarily the very popular netFPGA. I am following closely the evolution of development tools for large scale software development,i am open to new approaches and have a broad overview of what is available, what are the main tradeoffs (mainly rapid development vs Performance) and have experience in selecting the right tool for each job.

Notable Open Source Contributions

BlackAdder Network Node System

An alternative fully functional linux kernel network stack architecture for the Internet. Contributed to several subsystems.

The design is documented in this Paper: G. Parisis, D. Trossen and D. Syrivelis, Implementation and evaluation of an information-centric network, IFIP Networking 2013 (Presented Core System of the Pursuit project which won 2013 FIA award)

IPsec support for the MIT Click modular router project

In this work i have added IPsec support to the MIT Click Modular Router Project. The implementation and how-to can be found in Click-Wiki. This work was a result of my collaboration with Princeton University.

Volatile Software Suspend for uClinux kernel

In this work, i have developed a custom, first stage loader assisted, suspend-resume scheme for uClinux kernel. The system suspends execution by storing the H/w state in volatile memory. The overall process is extremely fast and the kernel is able to resume execution on adifferent H/w platform. On reconfigurable hardware platforms this is indeed useful. This work is backed up by a USENIX Annual Technical Conference Publication.

Porting the Blob bootloader and linux kernel to NMI uEngine platform

This work is now outdated because the hardware platform is outdated. It was done back in 2003 for the IST project 2WEAR. Patches have been integrated to the 2.4 series mainline Arm linux kernel.
